Why Community-Owned Solar?

• Access for All

Community solar lets renters, low-income households, and those with unsuitable roofs access solar energy benefits without installing panels on their own home.

• Lower Energy Bills

Subscribers typically receive a credit on their electricity bill that reflects their share of the solar energy produced, often saving 5–15% annually.

• Grid Decarbonization

Community solar projects displace fossil-fuel generation at the grid level, contributing to state and federal clean energy mandates.

• Local Economic Benefits

Projects create local construction and operations jobs, and keep energy dollars circulating in communities rather than flowing to distant fuel suppliers.

Key Terms Explained:

Community-Owned Solar
A shared solar installation whose electricity is credited to multiple subscribing households or businesses, allowing them to benefit from solar without rooftop panels.
MW-AC / MW-DC
AC (alternating current) capacity is the power delivered to the grid after conversion by inverters. DC (direct current) is the raw output of the solar panels. AC is typically 10–20% lower than DC.
Interconnection Year
The year the project was connected to the utility grid and began producing power for subscribers.
Utility Type
Whether the project is administered by an investor-owned utility, a consumer-owned or municipal utility, or a rural electric cooperative — each with different ownership and governance structures.
